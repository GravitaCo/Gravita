# Gravita — Process

## Overview

Gravita's engagement follows a structured flow: Scoping → Roadmap → Discovery → Design & Build → Communication → Support. Every phase prioritizes transparency, collaboration, and real business outcomes.

---

## Phase 1: Scoping

The first conversation with a prospect focuses on understanding the real need — not the surface request.

### Key Questions — Branding Projects
- Are you driving towards a Series B?
- What are you wanting to communicate?
- Is this about perception and where the company is going?
- Are you evolving your brand or reimagining it?
- What are your USPs?
- What is your mission?

### Key Questions — Website Projects
- What are your key goals?
- What is the purpose and need within the team?
- Do you use the website for lead generation or is it brand presence and validation?
- What would be a key success over the next 12 months from the website?
- What are your USPs?
- Do you know what a true value proposition is? And are you conveying that to your audience?

### What We Cover in the Scoping Call
- Their short-term and long-term challenges
- The bigger business picture beyond "keeping up with trends"
- Real ROI expectations: "What would be considered a ROI on this project?"
- How we work — our collaborative nature
- Our systems for autonomy and speed to market
- How we develop clear value propositions (see Value Proposition Framework below)

---

## Phase 2: Roadmap

After the scoping call, we develop a **weekly roadmap** for the customer:
- What we will do each week
- What each week will cost
- Overall project length and total cost estimate

We encourage the customer to use this roadmap to **benchmark** other agencies they're talking to. We flag red flags:
- Quotes that are suspiciously low (on time or price)
- Quotes that are excessively high (on time or price)

This transparency builds trust and positions Gravita as the honest option.

---

## Phase 3: Discovery

### Workshop (½ day to full day)
Top-level coverage of all project aspects, goals, and potential solutions. This is where we gather all company collateral — sales decks, marketing content — to understand existing narratives, industry context, and audience.

### Documentation Produced

**Overview and Analysis:**
- Who is [company] and what do they do
- Brand problems (internal and external)
- Industry problem they're solving
- How they solve those problems
- What they believe will address their brand problem
- Heuristic analysis (if they have existing products)
- USPs
- Competitor analysis
- Audience personas
- SWOT analysis (if needed)

**Brand Narrative:**
- Mission
- Values
- Purpose
- Brand traits
- Culture

**Product/Service Statements:**
- Features and functionality
- How products/marketing website is structured
- What each section does

**Technical Planning:**
- User flows / sitemap
- Individual features
- CMS structure
- SEO and accessibility requirements
- Integrations

## Value Proposition Framework

This is a core part of how Gravita thinks and communicates. Most companies confuse three things:

### Brand Value (Surface Level)
What the brand stands for. Perception-driven, not outcome-driven.
- *Example:* "We provide honest and transparent support"
- *Example:* "We use 100% eco-friendly packaging"

### Benefits (Mid Level)
What happens when using a service or product. Directionally useful but vague.
- *Example:* "Save time on X task by using X feature"
- *Example:* "No more frustration — start to be more productive with workflows"

### Value Proposition (Deep Level — What We Push Clients Toward)
The actual business/customer outcomes. Specific, measurable, and tied to impact.
- *Example:* "Reduce operating costs by up to 35% annually by reducing inefficiencies and increasing production"
- *Example:* "Increase staff retention by 80% by removing tedious tasks and providing increased autonomy"

### The Three Categories of Value Propositions
1. **Monetary** — Direct financial impact (cost savings, revenue growth)
2. **Perception** — How the company is seen by market, investors, talent
3. **Retention** — Customer retention, staff retention, reduced churn

**When writing for Gravita or its clients, always push toward the Value Proposition level.** If a draft only reaches Benefits, rewrite it to include specific outcomes.
